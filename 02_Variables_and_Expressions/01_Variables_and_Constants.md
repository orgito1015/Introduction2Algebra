## 📖 Key Concepts

- **Variable** — a letter (e.g., `x`, `y`, `n`) that represents an unknown or changing value.
- **Constant** — a fixed value that does not change (e.g., `5`, `-3`, `π`).
- **Coefficient** — the number multiplied by a variable in a term (in `7x`, the coefficient is `7`).
- **Term** — a single number, variable, or their product (e.g., `3x`, `-2`, `y²`).

## ✏️ Worked Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** In the expression `5x - 3`, identify the variable, coefficient, and constant.

**Solution:**
- Variable: `x`
- Coefficient: `5`
- Constant: `-3`

## 🧪 Practice Problems

1. Identify the coefficient and variable in: `−9t`.

3. Write an expression with variable `m`, coefficient `−3`, and constant `10`.
