## 📖 Key Concepts

- **Factoring** — rewriting a polynomial as a product of simpler expressions.
- **GCF (Greatest Common Factor)** — the largest factor shared by all terms.
- **Factoring by grouping** — used for 4-term polynomials.
- **Factoring trinomials** — find two numbers that multiply to `ac` and add to `b` in `ax² + bx + c`.
- **Difference of squares** — `a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)`.
- **Perfect square trinomials** — `a² ± 2ab + b² = (a ± b)²`.

## ✏️ Worked Examples

### Example 1 — GCF

**Problem:** Factor `6x³ - 9x².`

**Solution:** GCF = `3x²`  → `3x²(2x - 3)`

---

### Example 2 — Trinomial

**Problem:** Factor `x² + 5x + 6`.

**Solution:** Find two numbers that multiply to `6` and add to `5`: `2` and `3`.  
Result: `(x + 2)(x + 3)`

---

### Example 3 — Difference of squares

**Problem:** Factor `x² - 16`.

**Solution:** `(x + 4)(x - 4)`

## 🧪 Practice Problems

2. Factor: `x² - 3x - 10`.
3. Factor: `9x² - 25`.
