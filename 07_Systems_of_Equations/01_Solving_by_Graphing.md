## 📖 Key Concepts

A **system of equations** is two or more equations with the same variables. A solution is a set of values that satisfies all equations simultaneously.

**Types of systems:**

| Type | Graphs | Solutions |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Consistent & independent** | Lines intersect | Exactly one solution |
| **Consistent & dependent** | Lines overlap (same line) | Infinitely many solutions |
| **Inconsistent** | Lines are parallel | No solution |

**Graphing method:** plot both lines and find their intersection point.

## ✏️ Worked Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** Solve graphically:  
`y = x + 1`  
`y = -x + 3`

**Solution:**  
Line 1 passes through `(0,1)` and `(2,3)`.  
Line 2 passes through `(0,3)` and `(2,1)`.  
Intersection: `(1, 2)` ✅

## 🧪 Practice Problems

1. Solve graphically: `y = 2x - 1` and `y = -x + 5`.
2. Without graphing, classify: `y = 3x + 2` and `y = 3x - 4`.
3. What does it mean when two lines overlap?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Intersection at `(2, 3)`
2. Inconsistent — parallel lines (same slope, different y-intercepts)
3. Infinitely many solutions — the equations are dependent.

</details>
