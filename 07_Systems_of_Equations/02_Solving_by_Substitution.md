## 📖 Key Concepts

**Substitution method:**

1. Solve one equation for one variable.
2. Substitute that expression into the other equation.
3. Solve the resulting single-variable equation.
4. Back-substitute to find the other variable.
5. Check the solution in both original equations.

## ✏️ Worked Examples

### Example 1

**Problem:** Solve:  
`y = 2x + 1`  
`3x + y = 11`

**Solution:**  
Substitute `y = 2x + 1` into the second:  
`3x + (2x + 1) = 11 → 5x = 10 → x = 2`  
Then `y = 2(2) + 1 = 5`  
Solution: `(2, 5)`

## 🧪 Practice Problems

1. Solve: `x = y - 3` and `2x + y = 9`.
2. Solve: `y = -x` and `3x - 2y = 10`.
3. Solve: `2x + 3y = 12` and `x = 6 - y`.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. `2(y-3) + y = 9 → 3y = 15 → y = 5, x = 2`; Solution: `(2, 5)`
2. `3x - 2(-x) = 10 → 5x = 10 → x = 2, y = -2`; Solution: `(2, -2)`
3. `2(6-y) + 3y = 12 → 12 + y = 12 → y = 0, x = 6`; Solution: `(6, 0)`

</details>
