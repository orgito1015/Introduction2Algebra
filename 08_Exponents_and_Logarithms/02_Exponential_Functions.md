## 📖 Key Concepts

- **Exponential function** — `f(x) = a · bˣ` where `b > 0` and `b ≠ 1`.
- **Base `b`:**
  - `b > 1` → exponential **growth**
  - `0 < b < 1` → exponential **decay**
- **y-intercept** — always `(0, a)`.
- **Horizontal asymptote** — `y = 0` (the x-axis).
- **Natural base** — `e ≈ 2.71828`; `f(x) = eˣ` is the natural exponential function.

## ✏️ Worked Examples

### Example 1 — Growth vs. Decay

**Problem:** Identify whether `f(x) = 3 · (0.5)ˣ` represents growth or decay.

**Solution:** Base `b = 0.5 < 1` → **exponential decay**.

---

### Example 2 — Evaluate

**Problem:** Given `f(x) = 2ˣ`, evaluate `f(3)` and `f(-1)`.

**Solution:**  
`f(3) = 2³ = 8`  
`f(-1) = 2⁻¹ = 1/2`

## 🧪 Practice Problems

1. Is `f(x) = 5 · (1.2)ˣ` growth or decay?
2. Evaluate `f(x) = 3ˣ` at `x = 0` and `x = 4`.
3. What is the y-intercept of `f(x) = 7 · 2ˣ`?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Growth (base `1.2 > 1`)
2. `f(0) = 1`, `f(4) = 81`
3. `(0, 7)` (when `x = 0`: `7 · 2⁰ = 7`)

</details>
